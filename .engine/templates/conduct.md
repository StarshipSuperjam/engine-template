## Rule

<One code of conduct is one plain-language behavioral stance, written to the operator in the first person ("I …") — what I will or won't do, every session, in this project. Add it in TWO places in a layer file (`.engine/conduct/defaults.md` for the engine's universal stance, or `.engine/conduct/operator.md` for this deployment's own): (1) an entry in the frontmatter `codes` list — `id: conduct-<slug>` (lower-case, hyphenated), `title: <short title>`, `status: active`; and (2) a `## <short title>` section below — its heading matching the title exactly — whose body is this rule. Keep it to one or two sentences, plain language, no engine or developer jargon. Conduct is posture, never a gate: never write a rule that purports to skip a review, auto-approve a change, treat built-in memory as authoritative, or otherwise weaken a guardrail.>
